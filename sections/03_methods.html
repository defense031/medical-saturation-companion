3 · The model

The forward surgical team is a waiting line in which the wounded can die before they reach a table. A capacity tally that asks only whether the team is over or under its daily load misses those deaths, because they happen inside the wait.

The model computes one number a planner can act on: the casualty rate above which the team can no longer keep up and patients begin dying in the queue, and how far each lever moves that rate. The threshold is a closed-form result from forty years of queueing theory, and a discrete-event simulation of the same node reproduces it. The interactive version lives in the Sim tab.

The abandonment queue and its overload limit

The forward surgical node, the Forward Resuscitative Surgical Detachment (FRSD), is a finite-capacity, many-server queue with abandonment. Wounded arrive at rate $\lambda$; the FRSD's two tables serve at a rate doctrine fixes (135 min/case, capped at 10 cases/24 h)ATP 4-02.25; a casualty who is not in service before the survival window closes leaves the queue by dying. That is the died-of-wounds mechanism, modeled as reneging, with hazard $\theta(t)$ from the TML+ mortality curvesTML+.

In the tractable reduction the node is the Erlang-A queue, $M/M/s+M$: Poisson arrivals, $s$ servers, exponential service rate $\mu$, and an exponential patience rate $\theta$ that stands in for the survival clockGarnett'02. Capacity is $s\mu$ and utilization is $\rho=\lambda/(s\mu)$. The abandonment fraction, our DOW, stays well defined as $\rho\to 1$ and beyond.

The threshold (Whitt, efficiency-driven regime)Whitt'04Whitt'06: $$\lambda^{*}=s\mu, \qquad \text{DOW}(\lambda)\;\approx\;\left(1-\frac{s\mu}{\lambda}\right)^{\!+}.$$ Below $\lambda^{*}$ the node keeps up and DOW $\to 0$. Above it, served throughput caps at $s\mu$ and the excess inflow $\lambda-s\mu$ dies waiting. So $\lambda^{*}$ is a sharp elbow in DOW-versus-$\lambda$, and to first order the curve is distribution-free: the shape of casualty arrivals and patience enters only through the realized wait. That holds even though real casualties and survival times are not exponential.

What the queue adds over a capacity tally. A capacity-versus-demand tally returns over or under. The queue returns four quantities that bear on the resourcing call:

  1. The shape. DOW rises as $\left(1-s\mu/\lambda\right)^{+}$, an elbow. The curve gives how many more die per increment of overload, so it tells a force designer whether 10% more capacity recovers 5% of the deaths or 30%.
  2. A bounded answer. A queue without abandonment either stays stable or grows an unbounded line. Because casualties leave the queue by dying, the death fraction stays finite and computable even when inflow exceeds capacityWhitt'06.
  3. The burst. A node under capacity on the daily average still saturates inside each pulse and recovers between themBahouth'24. The daily average misses those deaths; only time-varying arrivals capture themEick'93.
  4. The lever. Contested transport leaves both $\mu$ and $\lambda$ untouched and lengthens holding time, which never appears in demand-versus-capacity. The queue turns that delay into deaths per added day.

How evacuation policy enters. Theater evacuation policy sets the longest a patient is held before onward movement. A threatened forward leg or a saturated strategic pipeline lengthens that hold; a longer hold keeps casualties in the system and lowers the effective abandonment rate $\theta$, since more of the survival window is spent waiting. The Erlang-A elasticitiesWhitt'06b turn that into the days of holding the system absorbs before DOW steps up.

What the model returns.

  1. The binding constraint is operating-table-hours. Capacity is $s\mu$, set by surgery, so the threshold sits well below the bed-count intuition the force reports against (Hospital Center ≤240 beds, down from >13,000 in 1991)AHEC.
  2. A quantified golden-hour collapse: $\lambda^{*}$ is a specific casualty rate, set against observed LSCO rates.
  3. The evacuation-policy sensitivity: deaths per added holding-day, a number a planner can act on.

Refinements layer back on the realism the closed form abstracts away: $M/G/c+G$ service and patience (Hall service times, a severity-indexed survival clock); time-varying arrivals by modified-offered-loadGreen'07 for the pulses; severity classes as a priority/clearing modelArgon'08Mills'13. The MASCAL sibling (Q232) is the batch-arrival variant.

What's been tried, and the gap

Each existing approach answers part of the question and stops short of a threshold with a mechanism behind it. Capacity-versus-demand arithmetic gives a binary. Discrete-event casualty simulations (SIMEDIS / NATO Vigorous Warrior; RAND JMPT; NPS FST sims SIMEDISRAND'21) give one scenario's number at a time, which is why this study uses simulation to validate the law and the closed form to state it. Capacity optimization allocates resources without characterizing the cliffBouma'05. MEDEVAC dispatch MDPs put the server on the aircraft, where the bottleneck is transportJenkins'18. System-dynamics flow models average out the reneging elbow. The one analytic surgical queue is civilian and treats overflow as patients turned away at the door, with no doctrinal $\mu$Ferreira'24. Qualitative LSCO casualty-care work says the golden hour breaks but names no rateRemondelli'23.

No published model derives an analytic saturation threshold for the forward surgical node with the service rate fixed by published doctrine and died-of-wounds modeled as queue abandonment. Three search passes, including an NPS Calhoun REST-API sweep, found the queueing machinery, the simulations, and one civilian loss-model. Why it is still open. The problem sits between two communities that rarely talk. Army medical planners answer it with simulations that give one scenario's number and neither generalize nor explain. Queueing theorists built the abandonment apparatus for call centers, where reneging means a customer hung up. Identifying died-of-wounds with reneging connects the two, and two decades of theory then bear on a question the defense literature has only simulated. The contribution is that connection and the calibration to doctrine and observed rates on top of it. Healthcare queueing is otherwise a settled application areaGreen'06.
