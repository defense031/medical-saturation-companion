Policy lab · pin a baseline, then play; the model grades the marginal value

5Set your conditions (air superiority, load, batch) and pin them as the baseline. Then apply an intervention and read its marginal value: the change in mortality against that fixed baseline. Each policy is one model lever; the engine scores it, it does not choose for you.

The frontier · the policy ranking reverses as air superiority falls

5Each line is one policy's marginal value (mortality points saved versus doing nothing) swept across the air-evac fraction, your pinned baseline otherwise fixed. The curves cross: node-side levers (more tables, damage-control) save the most when the air-evac fraction is high and fall toward zero as it drops; transit and clock levers (prolonged field care, faster ground, whole blood) gain as it drops, because more of the dying then happens before the node. At the crossing the best policy changes. What to buy depends on whether you will hold the air.
